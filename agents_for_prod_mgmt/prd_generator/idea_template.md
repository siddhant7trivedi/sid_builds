# Idea Input Template

Use this structure to get the best PRD output. You don't need to fill every field — but the more context you provide, the sharper the PRD.

---

## 1. What is the product or feature?
> One or two sentences describing what you want to build.

**Example:**
> A Slack bot that monitors GitHub PRs and posts a daily digest to the team channel showing open PRs, blockers, and merges.

---

## 2. What problem does it solve?
> Describe the pain point. Who experiences it, how often, and what's the current workaround?

**Example:**
> Engineering teams lose track of stale PRs. Developers manually check GitHub daily, leading to missed reviews and delayed merges.

---

## 3. Who are the users?
> Primary user (who uses it most), secondary user (who benefits indirectly).

**Example:**
> Primary: Software engineers on teams of 5–20 people.
> Secondary: Engineering managers tracking team velocity.

---

## 4. What does success look like?
> What changes after this is built? Any metrics or outcomes in mind?

**Example:**
> PR review turnaround drops from 3 days to under 1 day. Zero stale PRs older than 48 hours.

---

## 5. Constraints or non-negotiables (optional)
> Tech stack, timeline, budget, compliance requirements, or anything the solution must/must not do.

**Example:**
> Must integrate with existing Slack workspace. No new tools to be introduced. Must work without GitHub admin access.

---

## 6. What's explicitly out of scope (optional)
> What should this NOT do in v1?

**Example:**
> Not a full project management tool. No Jira integration in v1. No mobile app.

---

*The more you fill in, the less the AI has to assume — and assumptions are where PRDs go wrong.*
