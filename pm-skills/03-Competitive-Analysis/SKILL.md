# Competitive Analysis Skill

## Purpose
Move beyond feature comparison tables to understand competitive positioning, moats, and strategic white space.

---

## Core Principles

1. **Direct + indirect** — Always analyze both direct competitors and substitute behaviors
2. **Positioning > features** — How competitors frame themselves matters as much as what they do
3. **Trajectory matters** — Where competitors are heading is more important than where they are today
4. **Find the white space** — The goal is differentiation, not just parity mapping
5. **GenAI lens** — AI competitors can shift rapidly; track model capabilities, not just UI

---

## Competitor Taxonomy

### Tier 1: Direct Competitors
Same buyer, same use case, same value proposition

### Tier 2: Adjacent Competitors
Same buyer, overlapping use case, different primary job

### Tier 3: Substitutes
Different tool solving the same underlying job (e.g., Excel vs. Airtable)

### Tier 4: Potential Entrants
Hyperscalers, platform players, or well-funded startups that could enter

---

## Analysis Dimensions

### Product Dimensions
- Core features and capabilities
- AI/ML capabilities (if relevant)
- Integrations and ecosystem
- UX/DX quality
- Pricing model and tiers
- Platform vs. point solution

### Business Dimensions
- Target segment (company size, industry, buyer persona)
- GTM motion (PLG, sales-led, channel)
- Funding / resources
- Customer base and notable logos
- Key partnerships

### Strategic Dimensions
- Stated positioning and messaging
- Apparent differentiation strategy
- Moats (data, network effects, switching costs, brand)
- Weaknesses and blind spots

---

## Analysis Frameworks

### Positioning Canvas
Map competitors on 2×2s across dimensions that matter to YOUR buyer:
- e.g., Ease of use vs. Depth of capability
- e.g., Enterprise-grade vs. SMB-friendly
- e.g., AI-native vs. AI-augmented

### Feature Comparison Matrix
Score competitors across must-have, differentiating, and delighter features

### Jobs Coverage Map
For each JTBD in your space, which competitors address it well/poorly?

## GenAI Competitive Landscape Specifics

- **Model dependency**: Which LLM do they use? Proprietary vs. API?
- **Fine-tuning / RAG**: Do they have domain-specific model advantages?
- **Data flywheel**: Do they get smarter with more usage?
- **Prompt IP**: Is their system prompt a moat, or easily replicable?
- **Hyperscaler risk**: Could OpenAI, Google, or Microsoft build this natively?
- **Evaluation quality**: How do they ensure output quality? That's a differentiator.

---

## Research Sources

- Product websites and pricing pages
- G2, Capterra, Trustpilot reviews
- LinkedIn (hiring signals reveal strategic direction)
- Job postings (what they're building next)
- Earnings calls (if public)
- Twitter/X, podcasts (founder messaging)
- Teardowns and review articles
- App stores (feature updates)

---

## Output Standards

Every competitive analysis should produce:
1. Competitor landscape map (tiered)
2. Feature/positioning comparison matrix
3. Our differentiation hypothesis
4. White space opportunities
5. Risks and threats to monitor

---

## Quality Checks

- [ ] All four competitor tiers considered
- [ ] Analysis includes trajectory, not just current state
- [ ] Positioning language (their own words) captured
- [ ] White space is explicit, not just implied
- [ ] GenAI-specific moats assessed if in AI market
- [ ] "So what for us" is clearly stated per competitor
